# Swedish Copywriting: Avoiding AI-Swedish

## The Problem with AI-Swedish

AI-generated Swedish has its own set of tells beyond the English patterns. It often reads like translated English, uses overly formal constructions, and misses the natural casualness of how Swedes actually write marketing copy.

## Swedish-Specific AI Tells

### Translated English Structures
AI thinks in English and translates. Watch for:

❌ "Vi är stolta att presentera..." (We are proud to present)
✅ "Nytt från oss:" or just start with what you're presenting

❌ "Upplev skillnaden med..." (Experience the difference with)
✅ Describe the actual difference. Don't tell people to experience it.

❌ "Ta ditt [X] till nästa nivå" (Take your X to the next level)
✅ Say what actually happens. "Nästa nivå" means nothing.

❌ "I en värld där..." (In a world where...)
✅ Same rule as English. Delete and start with the interesting thing.

### Over-Formal Swedish
AI defaults to a formality level that's too high for Swedish marketing/web copy. Swedish commercial writing is generally more casual than English:

❌ "Vi erbjuder en omfattande lösning för era övervakningsbehov."
✅ "Vi håller koll på dina servrar. Dygnet runt."

❌ "Tveka inte att kontakta oss för ytterligare information."
✅ "Frågor? Hör av dig."

❌ "Det är vår övertygelse att kvalitet är av yttersta vikt."
✅ "Vi bakar med Valrhona-choklad. Punkt."

### Ni vs Du
Modern Swedish marketing uses almost exclusively "du" (singular you), not "ni" (plural/formal you). AI sometimes uses "ni" or "er" when it should use "du" and "din." The only exception is when specifically addressing a company/organization in B2B communication — and even then, "du" is increasingly common.

### The Swedish Buzzword Problem
Swedish AI copy has its own buzzword clusters:

**Avoid:** skräddarsydd (unless you literally tailor), banbrytande, innovativ, unik (everyone says it), passion/passionerad, i framkant, hållbar (unless specifically about sustainability), sömlös, nästa generations

**Use instead:** specific descriptions of what you actually do. "Skräddarsydd" → "Vi designar varje tårta från scratch — ingen mall, inget standardutbud." "Innovativ" → describe the actual innovation.

## What Good Swedish Copy Sounds Like

Swedish marketing copy at its best is:
- **Direct.** Swedes appreciate getting to the point.
- **Understated.** Slightly less hype than American copy. Confidence without chest-beating.
- **Personal.** "Du"-form, conversational, almost like talking to a friend.
- **Specific.** Swedish consumers are detail-oriented. Specifications, ingredients, process descriptions.
- **Wry.** Swedish humor in copy is dry and understated. A small joke earns more trust than enthusiasm.

### Examples by Context

**SaaS / Tech:**
❌ "Revolutionera din infrastrukturövervakning med vår banbrytande plattform."
✅ "Dina servrar kraschar ibland. Vi ser till att du vet om det före dina kunder."

**Food / Bakery:**
❌ "Vi brinner för att skapa unika kulinariska upplevelser med de finaste ingredienserna."
✅ "Smöret är Normandie-importerat, mjölet stenmalt från ett lokalt kvarneri, chokladen Valrhona. Resten är hantverk och envishet."

**Professional Services:**
❌ "Med mångårig erfarenhet och djup expertis erbjuder vi skräddarsydda lösningar."
✅ "Vi har drivit upphandlingsärenden sedan 2012. Vet vilka misstag kommuner gör och hur man hittar dem."

**E-commerce:**
❌ "Upptäck vårt noggrant utvalda sortiment av premiumprodukter."
✅ "47 produkter. Varje en testad och utvald av oss personligen. Vi säljer inget vi inte själva hade köpt."

## Swedish Rhythm

Swedish prose has a different natural rhythm than English. Some notes:

- Swedish tolerates longer sentences than English (compound sentences with commas are natural)
- BUT short sentences for emphasis work even better in Swedish because they contrast with the normally longer structure
- Swedish compound words (sammansättningar) create dense, specific terms — use this as a feature, not a bug: "bröllopstårtedesign" is one word that communicates immediately
- Alliteration and internal rhyme work well in Swedish headlines: "Tårtor som talar" / "Bakom varje bulle"

## The Du-Test

Read your Swedish copy and replace every instance of "du/din/dig" with a specific person's name (like "Erik" or "Anna"). Does it still sound natural? If it sounds weird with a name, the copy is too formal or too generic. Good Swedish copy should feel like you're talking to one person.
