# Search Intent Analysis

Search intent is the single most important factor in SEO. If you misunderstand WHY someone is searching, it doesn't matter how well-written your content is — it will never rank.

## The Four Types of Search Intent

### 1. Informational — "I want to LEARN"

The searcher wants knowledge. They're not ready to buy.

**Signals:**
- Questions: "how to," "what is," "why does," "when should"
- General terms: "plumbing repair guide," "lawn care tips," "sourdough starter"
- Problem-oriented: "kitchen faucet leaking," "brown spots on lawn," "bread not rising"

**What Google shows:** Blog posts, guides, how-to articles, videos, featured snippets, People Also Ask.

**What you should create:** In-depth guides, how-to content, explainer articles, FAQ pages.

**Example:**
- Search: "how to unclog a drain without chemicals"
- Intent: Learn a DIY method
- Best format: Step-by-step guide with images, video embed, list of tools needed
- WRONG: A service page for your plumbing company

### 2. Commercial Investigation — "I want to COMPARE"

The searcher knows they need something but hasn't decided what or from whom.

**Signals:**
- Comparisons: "best," "vs," "review," "top 10," "cheapest"
- Price-oriented: "cost of," "pricing," "how much does"
- Evaluation: "pros and cons," "is it worth it," "alternatives to"

**What Google shows:** Comparison articles, review roundups, "best of" lists, pricing pages.

**What you should create:** Comparison guides, pricing pages, case studies, testimonials.

**Example:**
- Search: "plumber vs handyman for bathroom remodel"
- Intent: Understand which professional to hire
- Best format: Comparison article: when to hire a plumber vs. a handyman, price differences, scope of work
- WRONG: A generic "About Us" page

### 3. Transactional — "I want to DO/BUY"

The searcher is ready to take action. Buy, book, sign up, download.

**Signals:**
- Action words: "buy," "order," "book," "hire," "schedule," "near me"
- Specific products/services: "emergency plumber Denver," "wedding cake Portland," "tax accountant Austin"
- Brand + action: "QuickBooks login," "Home Depot online order"

**What Google shows:** Product pages, service pages, Google Business Profiles, ads, booking widgets.

**What you should create:** Service pages, product pages, landing pages with clear CTAs, booking forms.

**Example:**
- Search: "emergency plumber Denver"
- Intent: Hire a plumber RIGHT NOW
- Best format: Service page with phone number, service area, pricing, reviews, and a booking form above the fold
- WRONG: A blog post about "What to do when your pipes burst"

### 4. Navigational — "I want to FIND a specific site"

The searcher wants a specific website or page.

**Signals:**
- Brand names: "Yelp plumber reviews," "Angi plumbing services"
- Specific pages: "QuickBooks login," "Google Business Profile setup"
- URL-like: "yellowpages.com Denver plumbers"

**What Google shows:** The specific site, its subpages, sitelinks.

**What you should create:** Make sure YOUR brand name leads to YOUR site. Optimize your homepage and key landing pages.

**Example:**
- Search: "Yelp plumber reviews Denver"
- Intent: Find plumber reviews on Yelp specifically
- You can't really "optimize" for this unless you ARE Yelp, but you CAN make sure "YourBrand plumber" leads to your site

## How to Analyze Search Intent in Practice

### Step 1: Google It

Seriously. Google your target keyword and look at what ranks. Google has already done the intent analysis for you through billions of data points.

**Look at:**
- **Type of results:** Are they blog posts? Product pages? Local pack? Videos?
- **Featured snippets:** Is Google answering the question directly? What format (list, paragraph, table)?
- **People Also Ask:** What related questions does Google show? These reveal the intent cluster.
- **Ads:** Are there ads? Many ads = high commercial intent.
- **Local pack:** Is there a map with local businesses? = Local intent.

### Step 2: Classify the Intent

For EVERY keyword you target, write down:

```
Keyword: [keyword]
Primary intent: [informational / commercial / transactional / navigational]
Secondary intent: [if mixed]
SERP features: [featured snippet / local pack / videos / PAA / ads]
Content format: [guide / service page / comparison / landing page]
```

### Step 3: Map Intent to Content Type

| Intent | Content Type | Goal |
|--------|-------------|------|
| Informational | Blog post, guide, FAQ | Build authority, attract top-funnel traffic |
| Commercial | Comparison, review, pricing page | Help decision-making, capture mid-funnel |
| Transactional | Service page, product page, landing page | Convert, capture bottom-funnel |
| Navigational | Homepage, brand page | Be findable for your brand |

## Common Intent Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Blog Post for Transactional Intent

**Search:** "plumber Denver"
**Wrong:** Blog post: "10 Reasons You Need a Plumber in Denver"
**Right:** Service page: "Licensed Plumber in Denver — Same-Day Service. Call Now."

Google shows service pages and local results for this search, not blog posts. Writing a blog post is wasted effort.

### Mistake 2: Service Page for Informational Intent

**Search:** "how to fix a running toilet"
**Wrong:** Service page: "Our Toilet Repair Services"
**Right:** How-to guide: "How to Fix a Running Toilet in 5 Steps (With Photos)"

The searcher wants to learn, not hire someone (yet). But a good guide builds trust — and you can add a CTA at the bottom: "Too complex? Call us for professional help."

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Mixed Intent

**Search:** "wedding photographer Austin"
**Wrong:** Only a portfolio page
**Right:** Portfolio page WITH pricing info, package comparisons, and testimonials

This search has both transactional intent (hire a photographer) and commercial investigation (compare photographers). Your page needs to satisfy both.

### Mistake 4: Same Content for Different Intents

**Search 1:** "landscaping cost"
**Search 2:** "landscaping company near me"
**Search 3:** "how to design a backyard"

These are three completely different intents that need three completely different pages. Many businesses try to target all three with a single "Landscaping Services" page. That page will rank for none of them.

## Intent Clustering

Group keywords by intent to avoid cannibalization (multiple pages competing for the same search):

**Cluster example: Plumbing in Denver**

```
TRANSACTIONAL CLUSTER (1 service page):
- plumber Denver
- plumbing service Denver
- Denver plumber near me
- emergency plumber Denver CO
- licensed plumber Denver

COMMERCIAL CLUSTER (1 pricing/comparison page):
- plumber cost Denver
- how much does a plumber charge in Denver
- affordable plumber Denver
- plumber Denver prices

INFORMATIONAL CLUSTER (multiple blog posts):
- how to find a good plumber in Denver
- plumbing code requirements Colorado
- common plumbing problems in old Denver homes
- when to call a plumber vs DIY
```

Each cluster gets ONE page. Not one page per keyword — one page per intent cluster. This prevents your own pages from competing against each other.

## Local Intent Signals

For service businesses, local intent is gold. These searches have high conversion rates.

**Strong local signals:**
- "[service] [city]" — "plumber Denver"
- "[service] near me" — "plumber near me"
- "[service] in [neighborhood]" — "plumber in Capitol Hill"
- "[service] open now" — "plumber open now"

**For local intent, prioritize:**
1. Google Business Profile (most important)
2. Service page with local signals (city name, neighborhood names, service area)
3. Local landing pages for each service area
4. Reviews and citations on Yelp, Yellow Pages, BBB, industry directories
