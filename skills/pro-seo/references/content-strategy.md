# Content Strategy

Content strategy is not "write blog posts about things related to your business." It's a structured plan for which content to create, in what order, targeting which searches, and how it all connects.

## Topic Clusters

The foundation of modern SEO content strategy. Instead of standalone articles, you build interconnected clusters of content around core topics.

### Structure

```
                    PILLAR PAGE
                  "Plumbing Services
                     in Denver"
                   /      |      \
                  /       |       \
    CLUSTER      CLUSTER      CLUSTER
    "Emergency    "Bathroom     "Pipe
     Plumbing"    Remodeling"   Repair"
    /    \        /    \        /    \
  blog   blog   blog   blog   blog   blog
  posts  posts  posts  posts  posts  posts
```

**Pillar page:** Comprehensive overview of the main topic. Long, authoritative, covers everything at a high level. Links to all cluster pages.

**Cluster pages:** Deep dives into subtopics. Each one links back to the pillar page AND to other relevant cluster pages.

**Why this works:** Google sees the interconnected structure and understands that your site has deep expertise on the topic. Internal links distribute authority. Users find everything they need.

### Example: Landscaping Company in Portland

**Pillar page:** "Complete Guide to Landscaping in Portland" (3,000+ words)
- Overview of Portland's climate zones and growing seasons
- Types of landscaping services
- Cost ranges
- How to choose a landscaping company
- Links to all cluster content

**Cluster 1: Lawn Care**
- "Best Grass Types for Portland's Climate"
- "Portland Lawn Care Schedule: Month-by-Month Guide"
- "How Much Does Lawn Care Cost in Portland?"
- "Organic Lawn Care: A Portland Homeowner's Guide"

**Cluster 2: Hardscaping**
- "Patio Design Ideas for Pacific Northwest Homes"
- "Retaining Walls in Portland: Materials, Costs, and Permits"
- "Driveway Options for Portland's Rainy Climate"

**Cluster 3: Garden Design**
- "Native Plants for Portland Gardens"
- "Rain Garden Installation: Portland's Eco-Friendly Solution"
- "Year-Round Color: Seasonal Planting Guide for Portland"

**Cluster 4: Tree Services**
- "Tree Removal in Portland: Permits, Costs, and When You Need It"
- "Best Shade Trees for Portland Properties"
- "Storm Damage Tree Care: What Portland Homeowners Need to Know"

## Prioritization Matrix

Not all content is equally valuable. Prioritize based on four factors:

### The Matrix

| Factor | Weight | How to Assess |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Search Volume | 25% | Keyword tools (Ahrefs, SEMrush, Google Keyword Planner) |
| Relevance | 30% | How directly does it relate to your services? |
| Competition | 25% | How hard is it to rank? (Domain authority of current results) |
| Conversion Potential | 20% | How likely is a searcher to become a customer? |

### Scoring Example: Wedding Bakery in Portland

| Content Idea | Volume | Relevance | Competition | Conversion | TOTAL |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| "Wedding cakes Portland" | 8/10 | 10/10 | 7/10 | 10/10 | 8.7 |
| "How much does a wedding cake cost" | 9/10 | 8/10 | 5/10 | 8/10 | 7.5 |
| "Wedding cake flavors" | 7/10 | 7/10 | 4/10 | 6/10 | 6.1 |
| "How to bake a cake" | 10/10 | 2/10 | 9/10 | 1/10 | 5.3 |
| "Gluten-free wedding cakes Portland" | 3/10 | 10/10 | 2/10 | 10/10 | 6.5 |

**Insights:**
- "Wedding cakes Portland" is the top priority — high relevance, high conversion, decent volume
- "How to bake a cake" has huge volume but near-zero conversion potential — waste of time
- "Gluten-free wedding cakes Portland" has low volume but very low competition and high conversion — easy win

### Priority Order

1. **Quick wins:** Low competition + high conversion (niche service pages, long-tail keywords)
2. **Core pages:** High relevance + high conversion (main service pages, location pages)
3. **Authority builders:** High volume + medium relevance (informational content, guides)
4. **Nice to have:** Low priority on all axes (save for later)

## Content Gap Analysis

Find what your competitors rank for that you don't.

### Process

1. **Identify search competitors** (not business competitors — the sites that rank for your target keywords)
2. **Export their ranking keywords** (use Ahrefs, SEMrush, or similar)
3. **Filter for relevant keywords** you don't rank for
4. **Categorize by intent** (informational, commercial, transactional)
5. **Prioritize using the matrix above**

### Common Gaps for Service Businesses

| Gap Type | Example | Priority |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Location pages | Competitor has pages for each neighborhood | HIGH |
| Service-specific pages | Competitor has a page for each service type | HIGH |
| Pricing content | Competitor answers "how much does X cost" | HIGH |
| How-to content | Competitor has DIY guides that build trust | MEDIUM |
| Comparison content | Competitor has "us vs. them" or "option A vs. B" | MEDIUM |
| Seasonal content | Competitor has seasonal guides (winter prep, spring cleaning) | LOW |

## Content Calendar

Plan content production realistically based on available resources.

### For 1 Article Per Month (Minimal Budget)

**Month 1-3:** Core service pages (transactional intent)
- Month 1: Main service page
- Month 2: Top secondary service page
- Month 3: Location-specific landing page

**Month 4-6:** Commercial investigation content
- Month 4: Pricing/cost guide
- Month 5: Comparison page (your service vs. alternatives)
- Month 6: FAQ page targeting People Also Ask

**Month 7-12:** Informational content
- Monthly blog posts targeting informational keywords with highest conversion potential

### For 1 Article Per Week (Moderate Budget)

**Week 1:** Service/transactional page
**Week 2:** Informational blog post
**Week 3:** Commercial investigation content
**Week 4:** Local/seasonal content

Rotate this pattern, prioritizing from the matrix.

### For Daily Content (High Budget)

Don't. Quality over quantity. 3-4 exceptional articles per week beats 7 mediocre ones. Use the extra days for updating existing content, building backlinks, and technical improvements.

## Content Updates

Existing content degrades over time. Plan regular updates:

### Update Schedule

| Content Type | Update Frequency | What to Update |
|---|---|---|
| Service pages | Quarterly | Pricing, services offered, testimonials |
| Pricing guides | Every 6 months | Current rates, new options |
| How-to guides | Annually | Check accuracy, add new methods |
| Seasonal content | Annually (before season) | Update dates, refresh examples |
| Comparison content | Every 6 months | New competitors, changed features |

### Signs Content Needs Updating

- Rankings dropping for target keyword
- Traffic declining on the page
- Bounce rate increasing
- Information is outdated (old prices, discontinued services)
- Competitors have published better content on the same topic

## Internal Linking Strategy

Internal links are the circulatory system of your site's SEO.

### Rules

1. **Every page links to the pillar page** of its cluster
2. **Pillar pages link to all cluster pages**
3. **Cluster pages link to related cluster pages** (across clusters too)
4. **Use descriptive anchor text** — "our Denver plumbing services" not "click here"
5. **New content always gets 3-5 internal links** from existing content
6. **High-authority pages link to pages you want to boost**

### Example Internal Link Map

```
Homepage
├── /services/plumbing/ (pillar)
│   ├── /services/plumbing/emergency/ → links to: pillar, /blog/burst-pipe-guide
│   ├── /services/plumbing/drain-cleaning/ → links to: pillar, /blog/clogged-drain-tips
│   └── /services/plumbing/water-heater/ → links to: pillar, /blog/water-heater-guide
├── /blog/burst-pipe-guide → links to: /services/plumbing/emergency/, pillar
├── /blog/clogged-drain-tips → links to: /services/plumbing/drain-cleaning/, pillar
└── /blog/water-heater-guide → links to: /services/plumbing/water-heater/, pillar
```

Every blog post drives authority to the service pages. Every service page links to relevant educational content. The pillar page ties everything together.

## Cannibalization Prevention

Content cannibalization happens when multiple pages on your site compete for the same keyword.

### How to Detect

- Search `site:yourdomain.com "target keyword"` — if multiple pages appear, you might have cannibalization
- Check Google Search Console for keywords where multiple pages appear

### How to Prevent

1. **One intent cluster = one page.** Never create two pages targeting the same intent.
2. **Use the intent clustering from search-intent.md** to group keywords.
3. **If cannibalization exists:** Merge the pages, 301 redirect the weaker one to the stronger one.
4. **Differentiate similar pages** with distinct intent angles (e.g., "plumber cost Denver" vs. "affordable plumber Denver" can be on the same page, but "plumber Denver" and "plumbing services Denver" should DEFINITELY be the same page).
