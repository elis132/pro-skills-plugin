# SEO Critic

Review every SEO strategy, piece of content, or technical implementation against this checklist. Be brutally honest. Bad SEO wastes time and money.

## Strategy Review

### 1. Search Intent Alignment

- [ ] Has search intent been analyzed for EVERY target keyword?
- [ ] Does each piece of content match its target intent (informational, commercial, transactional, navigational)?
- [ ] Have you actually Googled the target keywords and looked at what ranks?

**Red flag:** Creating a blog post for "plumber Denver." Google shows service pages and the local pack for this search, not blog posts. A blog post will never rank here.

**Red flag:** Creating a service page for "how to fix a leaky faucet." Google shows how-to guides. A service page won't match intent.

### 2. Topic Cluster Integrity

- [ ] Is there a clear pillar page for each core topic?
- [ ] Do cluster pages link back to the pillar?
- [ ] Does the pillar link to all cluster pages?
- [ ] Are cluster pages internally linked to each other where relevant?
- [ ] Is there exactly ONE page per intent cluster (no cannibalization)?

**Red flag:** Multiple blog posts targeting the same keyword. For example, "Plumber Cost in Denver" AND "How Much Does a Plumber Charge in Denver?" — these target the same intent and will cannibalize each other. Merge them.

**Red flag:** Pillar page has no internal links to cluster content. The whole structure falls apart.

### 3. Prioritization

- [ ] Are high-conversion, low-competition keywords prioritized first?
- [ ] Are quick wins identified (existing pages that need small improvements)?
- [ ] Is there a realistic content calendar based on available resources?
- [ ] Is the content mix balanced across the buyer's journey (awareness → consideration → decision)?

**Red flag:** Starting with high-volume, high-competition keywords. A new site writing about "plumber" (generic, massive competition) instead of "emergency plumber Capitol Hill Denver" (specific, low competition, high conversion).

**Red flag:** Only transactional content. Missing the 80% of searches that are informational or commercial.

## Content Review

### 4. Unique Value

- [ ] Does this content say something the top 5 results DON'T?
- [ ] Is there original data, real experience, or unique perspective?
- [ ] Would a reader gain something from this that they can't get elsewhere?

**Red flag:** The article reads like a summary of the top 3 search results. If you could generate this content just by combining existing articles, Google has no reason to rank yours.

**Red flag:** Generic statements like "hiring a professional plumber is a good investment." This adds zero value. Replace with specifics: "In the Denver metro area, professional drain cleaning runs $100-$350 depending on severity — compared to $2,000-$8,000 for water damage repair if a DIY fix fails."

### 5. E-E-A-T Signals

- [ ] Is there evidence of real experience (original photos, specific project details)?
- [ ] Does the content demonstrate expertise (technical depth, correct terminology)?
- [ ] Are trust signals present (business info, credentials, reviews)?
- [ ] Is there an author with real credentials?

**Red flag:** No original images — all stock photos. This screams "generic content farm."

**Red flag:** No specific numbers, measurements, or real-world examples. Just vague advice.

### 6. Content Formatting

- [ ] Is the H1 unique and contains the primary keyword?
- [ ] Are H2s and H3s used for logical structure?
- [ ] Are there tables for comparative data?
- [ ] Are lists used for scannable information?
- [ ] Are images original, compressed, and have descriptive alt text?
- [ ] Is the content length appropriate for the intent?

**Red flag:** A 3,000-word blog post for a transactional keyword. The searcher wants a phone number and a "Book Now" button, not an essay.

**Red flag:** A 500-word article for a comprehensive informational query. If the top results are 2,500+ words and cover multiple subtopics, your 500-word piece won't compete.

## Technical Review

### 7. On-Page Elements

- [ ] Title tag: 50-60 characters, keyword near the start, compelling?
- [ ] Meta description: 150-160 characters, keyword included, CTA present?
- [ ] URL: short, descriptive, includes keyword?
- [ ] Canonical tag: present and pointing to the correct URL?
- [ ] Schema markup: present, validated, error-free?

**Red flag:** Title tag is just the business name. "Denver Premier Plumbing" tells Google nothing about the page. Should be "Emergency Plumber Denver — 24/7 Same-Day Service | Denver Premier Plumbing"

**Red flag:** No meta description. Google will auto-generate one, and it's usually terrible.

### 8. Page Speed

- [ ] LCP under 2.5 seconds?
- [ ] INP under 200ms?
- [ ] CLS under 0.1?
- [ ] Images in WebP format and compressed?
- [ ] No render-blocking JavaScript?

**Red flag:** Loading a 5MB hero image. Compress it to WebP and it'll be 200KB.

**Red flag:** 15 third-party scripts (analytics, chat widgets, social media embeds) killing load time. Each one adds latency.

### 9. Mobile

- [ ] Site is responsive?
- [ ] Text readable without zooming?
- [ ] Buttons large enough to tap?
- [ ] No horizontal scrolling?

**Red flag:** The phone number on the service page isn't clickable on mobile. For a service business, the phone number is the most important conversion element.

### 10. Crawlability

- [ ] All important pages in the sitemap?
- [ ] No important pages blocked in robots.txt?
- [ ] No orphan pages (important pages without internal links)?
- [ ] No broken links?
- [ ] Redirect chains resolved?

**Red flag:** The new service page you created has zero internal links pointing to it. Google may never find it.

## Local SEO Review

### 11. Google Business Profile

- [ ] GBP claimed and verified?
- [ ] Business name, address, phone (NAP) consistent across all platforms?
- [ ] Correct primary and secondary categories?
- [ ] Business hours accurate?
- [ ] Photos uploaded (storefront, team, work in progress)?
- [ ] Regular posts (at least monthly)?
- [ ] Review response strategy (respond to ALL reviews, positive and negative)?

**Red flag:** NAP inconsistency. The website says "123 Main St, Suite 4" but GBP says "123 Main Street #4" and Yelp says "123 Main St." Google treats these as potentially different businesses.

**Red flag:** No review response strategy. 50 reviews with zero responses looks like the business doesn't care.

### 12. Local Citations

- [ ] Listed on Yelp, Yellow Pages, BBB, industry-specific directories?
- [ ] NAP consistent across all citations?
- [ ] No duplicate listings?

## Common AI SEO Mistakes to Watch For

### Mistake 1: Keyword Stuffing Disguised as "Optimization"

> "Looking for a plumber in Denver? Our Denver plumber services offer the best plumber experience in Denver. As a Denver plumbing company, our Denver plumbers are ready to help with all your Denver plumbing needs."

This reads terribly and Google penalizes it. Use the keyword naturally, 2-3 times maximum in the body text. Use variations and related terms.

### Mistake 2: Generic "10 Tips" Content

> "10 Tips for Maintaining Your Plumbing"
> 1. Fix leaks promptly
> 2. Don't pour grease down drains
> 3. ...

This exists on 500 other sites. Google has no reason to rank yours. Instead: "Plumbing Maintenance in Denver: A Local Plumber's Guide to Preventing Cold-Weather Pipe Issues" — unique angle, local specificity, real expertise.

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Local Search

For a service business in a specific area, spending months on blog content while the Google Business Profile is incomplete. Fix GBP first — it's where 80% of local leads come from.

### Mistake 4: Writing for Google Instead of People

If content sounds robotic, forced, or stuffed with keywords, real people won't engage with it. Google tracks user signals (bounce rate, time on page, pogo-sticking). Content that people leave quickly won't rank long.

### Mistake 5: No Clear Call to Action

Every page should have a clear next step for the reader:
- Service page: "Call now" / "Get a free estimate" / "Book online"
- Blog post: "Need help? Contact us" / "Read our related guide" / "Download our checklist"
- Comparison page: "Get a quote" / "See our packages"

A page without a CTA is a dead end.

### Mistake 6: Neglecting Existing Content

The fastest way to improve rankings is often NOT creating new content — it's improving existing content that's already on page 2-3. A content refresh can take an article from position 15 to position 5 in weeks, while a new article might take months to rank at all.

## The Final Test

Before publishing anything, ask:

1. **Would I trust this content if I were the searcher?**
2. **Does this page answer the search query better than what currently ranks?**
3. **Is there anything on this page that ONLY we could have written?**

If the answer to any of these is "no" — go back and fix it.
